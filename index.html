Bevy Band

Breathalyzer Bracelet

What is Bevy Band?

Nearly 10,000 people die annually because of drunk drivers and over 2,000 people die from alcohol poisoning in the US alone. Our product’s purpose is to decrease the amount of drunk drivers and to act as a tool for increasing responsible drinking. Our intention is to allow users to track their blood alcohol content (BAC) throughout their night. Users will also be able to see their friends blood alcohol levels to help look out for one another. By having this interactivity amongst a group, the intention is for your friends to look at your alcohol level and be able to alert you when or before you’ve had too much to drink and vice versa. Our product will consist of a wearable breathalyzer with an “At Glance” screen, and an Android Mobile Application where all information will be viewed and stored.

The two components of Bevy Band are the breathalyzer bracelet and an Android phone to run the Bevy Band app. This is illustrated below.

RoBro

Wooden Robot Controlled by an Android Application

What is RoBro?

RoBro is a robot controlled by an Android app through Bluetooth. RoBro is made out of wood, gears and arduino parts. RoBro's features include movement in all directions (forwards, backwards, left, right), an arm that has two joints, a claw that can open and close and a roaming feature controlled by a distance sensor.

Process

This project was done for the Design Studio 1 class and we used the Scrum project management methodology to keep our group working towards one goal.

Final Product

RoBro Development Website

Our final touches on RoBro was adding break lights that light up when RoBro is not moving. The tools that we used to make RoBro are:

  • Arduino
  • Processing
  • Lego Technic
  • Wood

Maker Faire 2015, 2016 & Future Plans

RoBro was showcased at Maker Faire 2015 and 2016. Maker Faire is a diverse family-friendly showcase of makers, artists & organizations who create amazing things in celebration of the do-it-yourself spirit. We were sponsored by Carleton University to host a booth to show off RoBro.

We really enjoyed showing RoBro to all of the families and children that attended. From the reaction that we saw at Maker Faire - we have decided to continue working on RoBro. We would like to use RoBro to inspire children to explore S.T.E.M.

PERFIT

Online sizing and visualization tool for clothing

What is PERFIT?

PERFIT is a fitting application and sizing tool for online shopping that uses body scanning to get accurate body measurements and shows the user the look and feel of the clothing based on the measurements.

PERFIT is currently under development and will be completed by April 2018.

High Level Goals

The following are the high level goals for PERFIT:

  1. Use technology to reduce the work the user has to do in current sizing solutions
  2. Create an accurate body scanning system for the clothing size predictions
  3. Build an aesthetically pleasing and intuitive interface that allows users to visualize clothing on their three-dimensional body
  4. Ensure the technological solution is or will be accessible to most users

Features

  • The body scan will produce anthropomorphic body measurements that will allow us to shape a virtual 3D version of the user’s body
  • Using a series of pre-made 3D modeled bodies and the body scan information, free-form deformation will be used to morph the model to the body scan measurements
  • A custom shopping platform with clothing options will be built to show users how the plugin could be used on other online shopping websites
  • The application will recommend the most appropriate size of clothing to best fit the user based on the body measurements
  • The selected clothing will be draped over the model to better represent the clothing using physics
  • The user will see a visual representation of what the clothing looks like on their body
